Chest

Intermediate

Bench Press

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Lie flat on the bench with eyes under the bar.
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  3. Unrack and lower the bar to mid-chest with control.
  4. Press up explosively until arms are fully extended.
Benefits
  • Builds raw upper-body pressing strength
  • Develops chest, shoulder, and triceps mass

Beginner

Push-ups

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Start in a plank position, hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor.
  3. Keep your core tight and elbows at roughly 45°.
  4. Push back up to the starting position.
Benefits
  • No equipment needed, trains anywhere
  • Builds functional pressing strength and core stability

Intermediate

Incline Dumbbell Press

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Set the bench to a 30–45° incline.
  2. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at chest level.
  3. Press both dumbbells up until arms are extended.
  4. Lower with control back to the starting position.
Benefits
  • Targets the upper chest more than flat presses
  • Improves shoulder stability through unilateral load

Back

3 exercises
Advanced

Pull-ups

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Hang from the bar with an overhand, shoulder-width grip.
  2. Engage your core and pull your chest toward the bar.
  3. Pull until your chin clears the bar.
  4. Lower with control to a full hang.
Benefits
  • Builds back width and grip strength
  • One of the best bodyweight back exercises

Beginner

Lat Pulldown

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Sit at the machine and grip the bar wider than shoulders.
  2. Lean back slightly and brace your core.
  3. Pull the bar down to upper chest level.
  4. Slowly return to the starting position.
Benefits
  • A more controlled alternative to pull-ups
  • Great for building back width as a beginner

Beginner

Seated Row

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Sit with feet braced and knees slightly bent.
  2. Grip the handle and start with arms extended.
  3. Pull the handle toward your torso, squeezing shoulder blades.
  4. Return slowly to the starting position.
Benefits
  • Builds mid-back thickness
  • Improves posture by strengthening the upper back

Legs

3 exercises
Intermediate

Squats

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, bar racked on upper back.
  2. Brace your core and bend at the hips and knees together.
  3. Lower until thighs are at least parallel to the floor.
  4. Drive through your heels to stand back up.
Benefits
  • The single best lower-body strength builder
  • Improves core stability and hip mobility

Beginner

Lunges

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Stand tall and step forward with one leg.
  2. Lower your hips until both knees are bent at ~90°.
  3. Push back through the front heel to return to standing.
  4. Alternate legs for the full set.
Benefits
  • Builds single-leg strength and balance
  • Helps correct left-right strength imbalances

Beginner

Leg Press

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Sit in the machine with feet shoulder-width on the platform.
  2. Release the safety bars and lower the platform with control.
  3. Lower until knees reach about 90°.
  4. Press back up without locking the knees fully.
Benefits
  • Loads the legs safely without balance demands
  • Good option when recovering from back strain

Shoulders

2 exercises
Intermediate

Shoulder Press

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Sit or stand holding dumbbells at shoulder height.
  2. Brace your core and press both weights overhead.
  3. Extend until arms are straight without locking elbows hard.
  4. Lower with control back to shoulder height.
Benefits
  • Builds overhead pressing strength
  • Develops shoulder size and stability

Beginner

Lateral Raises

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Stand holding a light dumbbell in each hand at your sides.
  2. With a slight elbow bend, raise both arms out to the sides.
  3. Lift until arms are roughly parallel to the floor.
  4. Lower slowly back to the starting position.
Benefits
  • Isolates the side delts for shoulder width
  • Low-load movement, easy on the joints

Arms

2 exercises
Beginner

Bicep Curl

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Stand holding a dumbbell in each hand, arms extended.
  2. Curl both weights up toward your shoulders.
  3. Squeeze the biceps at the top of the movement.
  4. Lower slowly back to the starting position.
Benefits
  • Classic isolation move for bicep size
  • Simple to learn, scales well with load

Beginner

Tricep Pushdown

View steps, benefits & muscles
Steps
  1. Stand at the cable machine, grip the bar with elbows tucked.
  2. Keep elbows fixed at your sides throughout.
  3. Push the bar down until arms are fully extended.
  4. Return slowly to the starting position.
Benefits
  • Isolates the triceps for arm definition
  • Easy to control, good for higher-rep finishers
